Patrick D. Cavanagh Ph.D.

Planetary Scientist | Software Engineer

About

I am a multidisciplinary planetary scientist investigating mineralogy and geochemical processes on the surface of Mars, Earth, and the Moon. My research focuses on better understanding the sulfur cycle on terrestrial planetary bodies through use of X-ray diffraction laboratory studies, stable sulfur isotopes, and spectroscopy. In addition, I look at the interaction of microbial life with minerals on the surface on Earth in extreme environments, such as Greenland and Antarctica, and its implications for the the survival of organisms on other planets.

I also have a deep passion for the intersection of science and engineering that occurs during mission design, planning, and operations. I am actively seeking opportunities to further my contribution to the future exploration of the terrestrial planets and ocean worlds.

Research Interests

  • Mineralogy - sulfates, clay minerals, and amorphous materials on Mars
  • X-ray diffraction studies
  • Stable Sulfur Isotope Geochemistry
  • Biogeochemistry
  • Remote sensing and VNIR spectroscopy
