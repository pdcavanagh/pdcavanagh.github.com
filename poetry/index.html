Take time

When would you like to schedule a followup
We are booked out
How's the 5th of November
I could do 4:15

That was the last conversation before
They took my vitals and made me wait
The results were in
But no one would tell me

I scrolled, past this past that
Dow down 100, S&P up 15
Inflation again, rates amiss
How long has it been

Taking the pen, cap off and on
It's a good thing my afternoon was clear
Swipe back up, no messages
Swipe left, swipe right

Deep breath
It really is taking a long time
I'm used to waiting
But come on

Still and shocked, knew it would be something
Like this
Fuck
Damnit

You're all set, do you want a reminder card
Just the copay is due today
I'm ok, I put it in my phone
Have a good day

I don't have anything to say to you right now
It's not your problem
I'll fucking figure it out
I'm sorry

The sun shone brighter that day
Witnessed the blue again
The way the cloud wisped across
No fluff, no bubble, just lines of faint

The car scorched hot, burning my hands
Metal clicking into its lock
Humid breath against my body
Just, just … is it though

A tear fell down my cheek
Or was it a bead of sweat
I lost my keys in my hand
And turned over the ignition

Turned over
Turned over

The car exploded into life, full air
Blew
Windows down and vents turned
Exhausted of excuses today

Back up, pull ahead
And I went
One more day, to think, or try not to
In this slowly dying body of mine

Sustain

We walked through those heavy doors
With a care on our mind
Slipping on the entry mat
Driving across the slick and grime

The umbrella stand to the left
The hat rack just beside
Those small offerings to greet our guest
Stay as long as you like

Swept up in the atmosphere
Cool wood tones and jolly tunes
What is this place we landed
Will there be candy or wine

A group we are not, we are something stronger
A stiff drink, a double, neat please
That's how we go together
Dressed up, no shorts, your slippers off

Drip down your legs with my touch
How smooth your skin, subtle, supple
Hold my hand, caresses over the knuckles
My eye following the seams of your dress

We floated out from the storm, into this paradise
Found each other again, in this paradise
You aren't really here, in this paradise
Just me, alone

in
this
paradise

Black

Will you come in that black dress
And drink a cup of black coffee
Against the view of seaside reflection
And shield ourselves in warmth

There is a little place, under a bridge
That serves the black beans of bliss
Poured into our cup, fuel for conversation
Let us go and meet, don't speak

A table in the back, velvet lined
Cushioned and cozy, you first
Laughter easy currency, a rich man
Dopamine floods and butterflies

She told me the story of her mother
Flying out against a city night
Up to something better, practitioner of dance
An old story of medicine and heart

How enraptured am I
My ego on display
Aware am I
My

I spoke of my father, corporate pillar
Saturday sessions, the suit left to dry
The evening brought music, cabled
Amplified, projected out into the alcohol soaked wood

Splash, crash, twirled, boom, brush
Pentatonic forces driving forward
Past the spectrum to single a source
The blues, beat up, black, and rusty

I lost you for a bit, perhaps ignored
Purposefully put you out, you stranger
Take away your power, remove your frets
Unplug your instrument, wield no more

How the attention roars back, back from the set
The dam opens and floods, mud first
Smoky eyes, bud light cans, bar stool plans
Don't slip now, no balance found, too much

Drive on, across that bridge, leave her there
Tears will surely fall, anger then rage,
Question me a thousand ways
And no such answers for the ride

The tape will turn over, reverse
I like this side for a time
Let it ride,
Let it ride

Tragedy

She screamed at the loss
Torn, cut, mangled, distraught
Her child was gone
And left only empty arms

A quiet stillness to the night before
Hot cocoa flowing free, laughter
Flickering candle lighting up the kitchen
Not so much, temperance, slow down
O, I suppose, she gives in to giggle and joy

Shattered sparklers on the floor
Green tinted, angular, dispersed
Flow of red cracked in the shield
Frantic, confused, deadened, and shocked

The smoke from the extinguished candle swirled above
What a good night, what fun was had
He's just so good in that movie
God I love him

Are you alright, are you ok?
Who are you trying to call?
Just stay still
Don't move

Let's read a bedtime story, or a show
Snuggle up so we can be warm together
Curled under this blanket
Want to play the stuffy game?

We are going to get you out
Just hang in there a little longer
Help is on the way
…

Just one more? This is the last
You need your rest
I promise
Oookay

I don't remember much after that
Fractured images of black and white
Handcuffed and screaming still
That pain, we need you to sober up

Child, are you okay?
She smiles, blinks, and flashes those eyes
Those bright blue eyes, those long lashes
I'm happy you are here

African flight


Will you let your hair go gray
And let it drip down your neck like pearls
Turning and curling to frame your gaze
A work, never finished, a state, grace

Will you join me on the balcony
And soak up the last sun rays
The cold night creeping in
The desert wind, fragmented sand

Will you let down your guard, that shoulder stress
Falling in with the pressure of my hand
Will you be safe, a sleep undisturbed
You'll know no love like, a thirst quenched

The compass dial, the sun, streams of satellites above
Help navigate our journey in, if we step
Your shawled silhouette, whispers of hair
Unravel in the blistering invisible force

The bed lies ahead, comfort all but promised
Leaving room for heartbreak, minor chance for failure
Catastrophic and complete, comatose and cold
But think not to that, that thought, that

Will you pause that twirl for just a moment
Just a moment to see
And scan your eye across the table
Just a moment to see

There's an ocean of comfort in those lost seconds
Where blue meets green
What is captured, quickly released
No cage, bars lifted, wind quickened

Will you share this moment with me
A desert dream
Unequivocal and hardly conceived, but
Just a moment, with me

Trash day

Loose strings of blue, wound tight against my hand
Constricting and restricting, bandaged, and planned
I swing open the door to humid air
Another world, wind rustling, quiet dampness
Swing past the dripping gutter, the depression of pavement
Round the red Prius, sunburnt and peeling
To the oasis of a nook, a holding pattern
A receptacle, to take away the clutter
There is a brief interlude, a respite, a space to escape
For nothing need be said, a tear easily shed
In this dark trash corner of mine
Swing the lid, release, the flow of blood touching the tips
And slam
The work is done, no reason to linger
Yet the quiet humid air beckons,
“Tell us secrets”

Strike out

Gently lifting my head turn
To the news, stocks dip,
Rub the dream from my eyes.
She's there again, walking away,
Purse pulled up, breath and courage.
She's still there, specter of morning light.
No need, no worry, the thoughts, just
Thoughts
But pull up, pull out, pull
This weight, the sour stomach cured with coffee
Pull up, pull out, put down
Those electrons
Swipe up, swipe down
Those thoughts of those
Not yours, not yours
I see it and she's gone, a door closed
A creak, an acknowledgment, a submission
Put down
And begin

Sometimes

Curved glass, cold in hand
Slip past your eye, sly, in demand
Swivel toward me, away from the flame
Little laughter in a pasta box
Separation by the partition, counter
Escape to a mirror, bathroom reflection
Long lines of sighs
Find me in the crowd
Hold my gaze a little while longer

…

Twisted hands, contorted face, hair pulling madness
Let it out my friend, scream, curl, crap, and put
Your love is the greatest
So bury it in a tomb
Your sadness has no match
Print it to a banner
I will fight til I make ___ my ___
That is what I said, there, standing in that bathroom
That mirrored profile staring back at me, questioning, my friend and enemy, my ego mine
